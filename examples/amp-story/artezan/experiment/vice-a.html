The Best Places to Drink in New York City

We all love our dive bars, but sometimes you just need a classy, stiff drink.

The beauty of New York is that you can nurse a tiki drink in someone's living room, then sneak into a hot dog joint speakeasy, all in one night. Ahead, the best cocktail bars in the city.

This place hits all the Brooklyn buzz-words: craft cocktails, DJs, and tacos without being a caricature. Order a frozen strawberry negroni.

Donna

📍27 Broadway, Brooklyn, NY 11249

This little Greenpoint restaurant is exactly the kind of place we love: strong rum cocktails, oysters, crispy chicken wings, an awesome burger, and some of the best people-watching in Brooklyn.

Alameda

📍195 Franklin St, Brooklyn, NY 11222

Achilles Heel

Andrew Tarlow's Greenpoint boozer would have made the area's dock workers of yesteryear proud with its seasonally-driven, delicious food from chef Lee Desrosiers.

📍180 West St, Brooklyn, NY 11222

Angel's Share

The bar that had a major hand in kicking off both the East Village speakeasy trend, and the city-wide obsession with Japanese mixology.

📍8 Stuyvesant St, New York, NY 10003

PDT

New York’s worst kept secret. The cocktail bar hidden behind Crif Dogs—accessible via phone booth—has has been serving great cocktails and chef-inspired hot dogs for a long time. PDT stands for “please don’t tell.” Oops.

📍113 St Marks Pl, New York, NY 10009

Night of Joy

This Williamsburg watering hole actually lives up to its name with an awesome rooftop and funky house-infused spirits (go for the beet and dill vodka). Inside, it's like  grandma's living room.

📍667 Lorimer Street,
Meeker Ave, Brooklyn, NY 11211

Ramona

This light and airy modern bar is the place to go for a “fancy cocktail.” Try the barrel-aged negroni. With its long white marble bar, it's a great place to drink alone without feeling like the degenerate that you are.

📍113 Franklin St, Brooklyn, NY 11222

Chilo's 

This Mexican theme bar stays crowded with great frozen drinks, outdoor space, and taco truck that turns out tortas and tacos late into the night. Michael Cera is one of Chilos’ co-owners.

📍323 Franklin Ave, Brooklyn, NY 11238
